My Background

I grew up in a household full of nerdy family members, and I'm no different. I was raised on the Nintendo Wii, the Xbox 360, and many other systems. I found real life to be plain and dreary, and so I let my imagination run wild. This helped me develop a love for storytelling in a short story format alongside poetry. I also had been labeled as a "Gifted Kid" at a young age, which led to much pressure alongside boredom. Yes, being intelligent may be a blessing, but it can also be a curse. People want to be your friend for the things you can do for them, and almost every other gifted kid was in competition with you. Social life was like a minefield. This is why I'm normally a loner. But thanks to a few new friends I've been getting to know very recently, I've begun to learn to embrace who I am as well as embrace relationships with my peers.


My Intellectual Fields

As with many geeks and nerds like me, there are always certain fields or special interest that we are more knowledgable in than most. For me, I tend to study mythologies of ancient civilizations. Greco-Roman, Egyptian, Norse, and even a little bit of Japanese! I plan to incorporate these mythologies into a future video game I'd love to publish. Now, onto more academic topics, I tend to excel in English and Math, but (non-ancient)history and science can sometimes be a bore for me. I have some other areas of random knowledge, but none are too extensive to where I'd say that my knowledge of those are on the level of my former fields of intellegence I have listed.


My Past Works
